# Bark & Stroll — Google Business Profile: claim & optimize

**Goal:** get seen when people search "dog walking pittsburgh," and be easy to recommend (a shareable Google pin + visible star rating).

**Key fact:** a listing already EXISTS — the site links to it: https://maps.app.goo.gl/zNLLyYJJ37fpXMNR7 and shows Google reviews. So this is **claim + optimize**, NOT create.
**Golden rule: never create a second profile.** Duplicates get suspended. Always work the existing one.

---

## Step 1 — Figure out who controls the existing listing
On your phone, signed into the Google account that should own this (likely scott@barkstroll.com):
1. Open the Maps link above, OR search "Bark & Stroll" / "Bridgeville Bark & Stroll" in Google Maps.
2. Look at the business listing for one of:
   - **"You manage this Business Profile"** → already claimed by you. Skip to Step 3.
   - **"Claim this business" / "Own this business?"** → unclaimed. Do Step 2.
   - **"Suggest an edit" only / "Request access"** → someone else (or an old account) claimed it. Click **Request access** and pick the account you want it under.

## Step 2 — Claim it (if unclaimed)
1. Tap **Claim / Own this business**.
2. Google will ask to **verify** — usually a short **video** (record the area you work / a branded item) or a **postcard** by mail. Follow whichever it offers.
3. Verification can take a few days (postcard) — that's normal. You can fill in details while it's pending.

## Step 3 — Set it as a SERVICE-AREA business (hides your address)
This matches your no-home-address rule.
1. In the profile: **Edit profile → Location/Address**.
2. **Turn OFF / clear the street address** ("I deliver goods and services to my customers"). Keep the city as Bridgeville for the region, but do **not** show a street address.
3. Set **Service areas** to the towns you actually cover:
   Bridgeville, Mt. Lebanon, Upper St. Clair, Castle Shannon, Carnegie, Scott Township, Collier Township, Heidelberg — and you can add "South Hills" / "southwest Pittsburgh."
   (Keep it to where you really drive — out-of-area folks still get filtered by the website form.)

## Step 4 — Categories (this drives what you rank for)
- **Primary category: Dog Walker** — most important field for "dog walking" searches.
- **Secondary:** Pet Sitter, Pet Care Service.

## Step 5 — Fill in the rest (match the website so signals agree)
- **Name:** use the real name only — "Bark & Stroll LLC" (or "Bridgeville Bark & Stroll" if that's your signage). **Do NOT** stuff keywords like "Dog Walking Pittsburgh" into the name — that's a suspension risk.
- **Website:** https://barkstroll.com
- **Hours:** Mon–Fri 8:00–6:00, Sat 9:00–4:00 (matches the site).
- **Services:** add Dog Walking, Pet Sitting, Drop-In Visits, Overnights, Pet Feeding.
- **Description:** something like — "Locally owned dog walking and pet care based in Bridgeville, serving the South Hills suburbs southwest of Pittsburgh. Free meet & greet, photo updates every visit, five-star reviews from neighbors."
- **Photos:** add several real shots (reuse the gallery photos). Profiles with photos get far more clicks. Add a few every couple weeks.

## Step 6 — Phone number: DO NOT add one
**Decision (Scott, 2026-05-24): no phone number on the Google profile.** All contact comes through the **website contact form** — leads reach out via the form first, and only then does Scott follow up. This matches the form-only model across the whole site (no telephone anywhere).
- In the profile, leave the phone field **blank** (ignore Google's "Add place's phone number" prompt).
- Make sure the **Website** field points to barkstroll.com so the profile drives people to the form.
- Trade-off accepted: no "Call" button. That's intentional — keeps all intake on the form.

## Step 7 — Reviews = the engine for "found → recommended"
This is what makes people trust you and what shows next to your name in search.
1. In the profile: **"Ask for reviews"** → **copy your review link** (a short g.page/... link).
2. Text it to recent happy clients — start with **Kara** and **Jackie** (already on the site), then every new client after a good week.
   - Sample text: *"So glad [dog] had a great week! If you have 20 seconds, a quick Google review really helps a small local business like ours: [link]"*
3. Aim for a slow steady trickle (a couple a month) — steady beats a sudden burst, and recency matters.

---

## What NOT to do
- Don't create a second/duplicate profile.
- Don't put a fake or shared street address.
- Don't keyword-stuff the business name.
- Don't buy reviews.

Combined with the website changes (done 2026-05-23), this is the full "get seen + easy to recommend" play. Movement typically shows over a few weeks as reviews and activity accrue.
