BARK STROLL LLC
Independent Contractor Agreement
Dog Walking & Pet Care Services · Commonwealth of Pennsylvania

This Independent Contractor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between Bark Stroll LLC, a Pennsylvania limited liability company ("Company"), and the individual identified in the signature block below ("Contractor"). The Agreement takes effect on the date Contractor accepts it electronically as described in Section 14.

1. Services

Contractor will provide dog walking, pet sitting, drop-in pet visits, and related pet care services ("Services") to Company's clients on an assignment-by-assignment basis. Each assignment is offered individually. Contractor may accept or decline any individual assignment without penalty and without any obligation to accept future assignments.

2. Independent Contractor Status

Contractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, joint venturer, or partner of Company. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employer-employee relationship.

  1. Contractor controls the manner and means of performing the Services, subject only to client-specific instructions and the conduct standards in Section 6.
  2. Contractor is responsible for all federal, state, and local income taxes, self-employment taxes, and any other taxes arising from compensation under this Agreement. Company will not withhold income taxes, FICA, Medicare, or unemployment taxes.
  3. Contractor is not entitled to and will not receive employee benefits, including but not limited to health insurance, retirement contributions, paid time off, workers' compensation, or unemployment compensation.
  4. Contractor will be issued IRS Form 1099-NEC for any calendar year in which compensation from Company meets or exceeds the then-current IRS reporting threshold (currently $600). Contractor will provide a completed IRS Form W-9 before the first paid assignment.

3. Compensation

  1. Standard rate: $20.00 USD per completed 30-minute visit, paid by Company to Contractor.
  2. Non-standard visit types (longer walks, overnight stays, holidays, etc.) will be quoted and agreed in writing before the assignment is accepted.
  3. Payment is made on a regular cadence communicated by Company, typically via Zelle, Venmo, or direct deposit. Payment timing is at Company's reasonable discretion but will not exceed 30 days from the date of each completed assignment.
  4. Contractor is responsible for all expenses incurred in performing the Services, including but not limited to vehicle costs, mileage, fuel, mobile phone, and personal supplies. No reimbursement is provided unless agreed in writing in advance.

4. Background Check

Contractor acknowledges that, prior to the effective date of this Agreement, Company obtained Contractor's written authorization to conduct a consumer report and/or investigative consumer report through a consumer reporting agency (currently Checkr, Inc.) in accordance with the Fair Credit Reporting Act (15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq.). Company reserves the right, upon reasonable notice and with renewed written authorization, to conduct periodic re-screenings during the term of this Agreement.

5. Insurance and Equipment

  1. Contractor will maintain valid automobile liability insurance at or above Pennsylvania state minimums for the duration of this Agreement, if Contractor uses a vehicle to perform any Services.
  2. Contractor will maintain a valid driver's license and a reasonably safe vehicle suitable for transporting animals when an assignment requires it.
  3. Company maintains general liability insurance covering its operations. Contractor is not a named insured under Company's policies and is not entitled to coverage as an employee would be.
  4. Contractor is encouraged, but not required, to carry an individual pet-care liability policy. Coverage is the Contractor's responsibility, not the Company's.

6. Conduct Standards

For every assignment, Contractor will:

  1. Treat every animal humanely. Any form of physical correction, striking, choking, kicking, or deprivation is grounds for immediate termination of this Agreement and may be reported to authorities.
  2. Follow the client's written care instructions (feeding, medications, leash handling, off-leash restrictions, etc.) as relayed by Company.
  3. Arrive within the agreed visit window or notify Company promptly by text if running late.
  4. Communicate exclusively with Company, not with clients. Contractor will text Company at +1 (412) 992-1480 at the start of each visit (arrival confirmation) and at the end of each visit (a brief status update and at least one photograph when reasonably possible). Company handles all communication with the client. Contractor will not contact clients directly by phone, text, email, or in-person conversation, except for unavoidable in-person interactions at pickup/drop-off, which must be kept brief and professional.
  5. Carry waste bags and properly dispose of pet waste during walks.
  6. Secure client property: lock doors, reset alarms, replace keys/lockboxes exactly as instructed.
  7. Refrain from operating under the influence of alcohol, cannabis, or any impairing substance.
  8. Refrain from smoking or vaping in or near client homes or vehicles.
  9. Promptly report any incident involving injury (to animal, person, or property), illness, escape, bite, or property damage to Company and the client.

7. Confidentiality

Client information (names, addresses, phone numbers, alarm codes, lockbox codes, key locations, household routines, animal medical details, and any personal information observed during Services) is the confidential property of Company and its clients. Contractor will not disclose, share, photograph for public posting, or use this information for any purpose other than performing the Services. This obligation survives termination of this Agreement indefinitely.

8. Non-Solicitation

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months after termination, Contractor will not, directly or indirectly:

  1. Solicit, accept, or perform any pet care services for any Company client whom Contractor served, met, or learned of through Company, outside of the Company's booking system; or
  2. Induce any Company client to terminate their relationship with Company.

If a Company client independently and without solicitation contacts Contractor for private services during this period, Contractor will (a) inform Company in writing within seven (7) days and (b) decline the engagement unless Company gives written consent. The parties agree this restriction is reasonable in scope and duration given the goodwill and client relationships Company invests in developing.

9. Photographs and Media

Contractor grants Company a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use photographs and short videos that Contractor takes during assignments for Company's marketing, social media, and website, provided the content shows only the pet (no client property identifiers, no client personal information). Contractor may not publish client-pet content on Contractor's own social media or other platforms without Company's prior written approval.

10. Equipment, Property, and Reimbursable Loss

If a key, lockbox code, or other client property entrusted to Contractor is lost, damaged, or compromised through Contractor's negligence, Contractor is responsible for the reasonable cost of replacement (e.g., re-keying, lockbox replacement). Company may deduct documented replacement costs from amounts owed to Contractor, with prior written notice and an itemized statement.

11. Indemnification

Contractor will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company, its members, officers, and agents from and against any third-party claims, damages, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of Contractor's negligence, willful misconduct, breach of this Agreement, or violation of any law in performing the Services. Company will indemnify Contractor on the same terms for claims arising out of Company's negligence or willful misconduct.

12. Term and Termination

  1. This Agreement remains in effect until terminated by either party.
  2. Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time, with or without cause, by giving written notice (email is sufficient).
  3. Company may terminate immediately for cause, including but not limited to: animal cruelty or mistreatment, theft, dishonesty, repeated failure to show up for assignments, breach of confidentiality, breach of Section 8, or any act that exposes Company or a client to legal or reputational harm.
  4. Sections 7 (Confidentiality), 8 (Non-Solicitation), 10 (Reimbursable Loss), 11 (Indemnification), and 13 (Governing Law) survive termination.

13. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. The parties will first attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute through informal negotiation. Any unresolved dispute will be brought exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania, and the parties consent to personal jurisdiction and venue in those courts.
